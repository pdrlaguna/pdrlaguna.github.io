As usual, the main goal of the design system includes improving UI consistency, quality and making the development process more efficient and focused.

Helps designers

Our research showed that Farmbox had a real issue with information density. Agronomists managing a farm usually worked in two modes.

Help the user

They're either following a routine…

Most of the days a farmer makes around 50 decisions. Needing for a that a UI that is fast, clear and simple. Any insights that we can surface does help, but speed has a way bigger impact.

Help the user

or they're managing a crisis!

Very few, extremely complex decisions to make. Happens a couple of times a year. They ask us for EVERY SINGLE DATA we could provide them to be glanced at, crossed with and analysed at the same time.

So we had some constraints…

Studying their decision making process during the harvesting season (with great insights from our support team) helped us figure out that what we needed was a responsiveness scheme focused on optimizing for information density and clear hierarchy.
‍
Possibility of having lots of data, labels and concepts to show and teach even on a small computer screen with clear hierarchy of what you should be doing at all the time

This work one day grew into soil.

A very strict system on a style level, with strict grid, color, type and components hierarchy — everything is blocked correctly and always has the proper white space around it.

My approach for creating it

1. Analyse the way the whole product team enjoys working

What is working really well that we should keep and how can we use this a chance to fix development problems? Some nice patterns were there, but not on the surface.

2. Cover all the basics as soon as possible

It has everything it should.

Figma library and code is the same

3. Hard constraints where everyone can agree on

Helps cascade style decisions down on the system: much like the development team is doing on CSS.
‍
Answering if an element can always be represented in the same way solves for faster production and faster development.

Makes sure the hierarchy model is always followed.

Principles first, tactics later!

1. It’s not a one size fits all. Sometimes a design solution might feel weird or wrong on a “common design sense”, but if we can prove it’s right for Farmbox, it’s right for soil too.
‍
2. Conventions over configuration. A designer only needs to specify unconventional aspects of the design — and should probably spend most of its time doing that!
‍
3. Flexible information density paradigm. It should be fine to have few elements on the screen, but it also should be straightforward to add many.

8pt grid system

Regular 8pt grid with everything trying to align top and left, but centered on itself. Was weird at first, but proved extremely powerful in letting designers push pixels until all data was shown and balanced.
